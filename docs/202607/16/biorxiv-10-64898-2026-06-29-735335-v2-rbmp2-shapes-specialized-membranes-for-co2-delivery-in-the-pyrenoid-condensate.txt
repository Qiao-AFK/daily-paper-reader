ABSTRACT 19 

Approximately one -third of global CO ₂ fixation occurs in the algal pyrenoid, a condensate of the 20 

CO 2-fixing enzyme Rubisco traversed by membranes that supply it with CO ₂. In the model alga 21 

Chlamydomonas reinhardtii , CO 2 is delivered by a specialized central reticulated region of these 22 

membranes . Here we define how this region is built and identify RBMP 2 as the first factor 23 

required for its biogenesis . Loss of RBMP2 prevents reticulated -region formation and impairs 24 

pyrenoid -dependent growth. RBMP2 domains perform separable roles in extending membrane 25 

tubule s toward the pyrenoid center and remodeling them into nar row tubules of the reticulated 26 

region . The CO 2-releasing carbonic anhydrase CAH3 localizes not only to the reticulated region 27 

but also to helical interface s between tubules and minitubules , which expand in some RBMP2 28 

truncation mutants . Our findings advance the basic understanding of pyrenoid function and 29 

establish the pyrenoid as a powerful system for studying membrane remodeling within a phase -30 

separated condensate. 31

INTRODUCTION 33 

Approximately one -third of global CO ₂ fixation takes place within the pyrenoid, an organelle 34 

found in the chloroplasts of many algae and some land plants 1–8. The pyrenoid consists of a35 

phase -separated matrix composed primarily of Rubisco, the CO ₂-fixing enzyme , and is 36 

traversed by membranes that deliver concentrated CO ₂9–14 . In the model alga Chlamydomonas 37 

reinhardtii (Chlamydomonas hereafter) 15 , the pyrenoid -traversing membranes (Fig. 1a) are 38 

required for growth under CO ₂-limiting c onditions 16 –18 , underscoring their essential role in the 39 

pyrenoid CO ₂-concentrating mechanis m.40 

41 

The Chlamydomonas pyrenoid -traversing membranes are form ed from thylakoid membrane 42 

sheets in the surrounding chloroplast that enter the Rubisco matrix and are reshape d into 43 

cylindrical tubules 16,18 . In this same zone , the inter -thylakoid stromal spaces are remodel ed into 44 

minitubules , smaller membrane tubes within the tubules 16 (Fig. 1b) . Toward the pyrenoid center, 45 

the cylindrical tubules converge and narrow to form a central reticulated membrane network 16 .46 

We recently found that th is reticulated region hosts the key carbonic anhydrase CAH3 (encoded 47 

by Cre09.g415700 )19 , the enzyme thought to convert HCO ₃⁻ to CO 2 to release CO ₂20 –22 ,48 

suggesting that this region functions as the central site of CO ₂ release within the pyrenoid. How 49 

this membrane architecture is built remains unknown. Understanding reticulated -region 50 

biogenesis is important for engineering a pyrenoid into crops to potentially increase yields 23 –25 ,51 

as correctly positioning CAH3 will require reconstructing the membrane environments that 52 

recruit or retain it. The reticulated region also provides a model for how membranes within 53 

condensates can be re -shaped to sp atially organize enzyme activity 26 .54 

55 

Here, we identify RBMP2 (Rubisco -Binding Membrane Protein 2, encoded by Cre09.g416850 ),56 

as the first known factor required for reticulated -region biogenesis . We show that RBMP2 loss 57

Loss of RBMP2 mislocalizes CAH3 and impairs growth under very low CO ₂70 

The predicted transmembrane protein RBMP2 localizes to the reticulated region 27 , similar to the 71 

CO ₂-releasing enzyme CAH3 19 , raising the possibility that RBMP2 mediates CAH3's localization 72 

to this region . We therefore used expansion microscopy (ExM) to ask whether RBMP2 is 73 

required for CAH3 localization. Consistent with previous observ ation s19 , CAH3 formed a 74 

network pattern at the center of wild -type pyrenoids, supporting its localization to the reticulated 75 

region (Fig. 1c , Extended Data Fig. 1a and Supplementary Video 1 ). By contrast, in the rbmp2 76 

mutant (LMJ.RY0402.215371 , PCR verification in Supplementary Fig. 1 ), CAH3 no longer 77 

formed a central network and i nstead mislocalized as puncta and streaks near the periphery of 78 

the Rubisco matrix (Fig. 1d , Extended Data Fig. 1b and Supplementary Video 1 ). 79

Overexpressing RBMP2 expands the reticulated region 112 

Having found that RBMP2 is required to form the reticulated region, we next asked whether 113 

overexpressing RBMP2 can promote the expansion of this region . In wild -type cells 114 

over expressing RBMP2 -Venus , we observed by ExM that RBMP2 -Venus localized throughout an 115 

enlarged reticulated region (Fig. 2g). We confirmed the presence of an enlarged reticulated region 116 

in this strain by TEM (Fig. 2h and Extended Data Fig. 2 b). Quantification of TEM images showed 117 

that the reticulated -region -to -matrix area ratio increased by ~50% in wild -type; RBMP2 -Venus 118 

cells relative to wild type (Fig. 2i , two -sided t-test , P = 0.0232 ). Thus, RBMP2 is not only required 119 

for reticulated -region formation ; overexpressing the RBMP2 protein is sufficient to promote the 120 

expansion of the reticulated region in wild -type cells. 121

Discussion 299 

In this study, we identif ied RBMP2 as the first known factor required to build the reticulated region, 300 

the membrane network at the center of the pyrenoid that houses the essential CO ₂-releasing 301 

enzyme CAH3. By uncovering how this region is built, our work advances the mechanistic 302 

understanding of pyrenoid membrane morphogenesis and contributes conceptual foundations for 303 

positioning CAH3 in efforts to engineer pyrenoids into plants. 304 

305 

Based on our findings, we propose a mechanistic model in which reticulated -region biogenesis 306 

proceeds via two separable RBMP2 -dependent steps: (1) extension of cylindrical tubules toward 307 

the pyrenoid center, and (2) conversion of cylindrical tubules into the narrow, minitubule -free 308 

tubules of the reticulated region (Fig. 6a –f and Extended Data Fig. 8m ). The phenotypes of the 309 

rbmp2; ΔMCP -Venus and rbmp2; ΔTM -Venus strains demonstrate that tubule extension can occur 310

A notable finding of this work is that a non -catalytic rhodanese domain can influence membrane 316 

morphology, revealing a previously unrecognized role for this protein family. Non -catalytic 317 

rhodanese domains are widespread across the tree of life, but their biological functions remain 318 

poorly understood . Our results suggest that the RBMP2 RHO domain promotes cylindrical tubule 319 

elongation, pointing to a possible role in membrane -architecture control. As this domain has been 320 

proposed to mediate protein –protein interactions 37 , it may promote cylindrical tubule elongation 321 

by recruiting one or more other factors in the pyrenoid. Our previous immunoprecipitation 322 

identified LCI16 as an interactor of RBMP2 38 , and here we observed a predicted high -confidence 323 

AlphaFold co -complex structure between LCI16 's N-terminus and RBMP2's rhodanese domain 324 

(Supplementary Fig. 3). However, the presence of a normal reticulated region in the lci16 mutant 38 

325 

suggests that LCI16 is not essential for reticulated -region biogenesis . Determining whether any 326 

other protein (s) act with the RBMP2 rhodanese domain to drive tubule elongation will be an 327 

important next step. 328

Our results suggest that the MCP and TM domains act to remodel cylindrical tubules into 330 

reticulated -region tubules. The MCP domain is predicted to be predominantly α-helical and to be 331 

in contact with the membrane, raising the possibility that it helps form or stabilize the high 332 

curvature of the reticulated region. The TM region contains predicted transmembrane helices on 333 

both sides of the RHO domain that may anchor RBMP2 to membranes, with the C -terminal helices 334 

potentially forming a membrane hairpin that may further help generate reticulated -region 335 

curvature 39 .336

Localization of CAH3 near the center of the Rubisco matrix is likely crucial, as this should increase 349 

the likelihood that released CO ₂ is captured by Rubisco before diffusing out of the matrix 24 . Our 350 

results suggest that CAH3 localizes not only to the reticulated region, as previously shown 19 , but 351 

additionally that it localizes to specialized portions of the cylindrical tubules, at the interface 352

which is also positioned close to the matrix center (Fig. 5m –v and Extended Data Fig. 9). This 354 

observation raises the question of what, if any, is the functional distinction between these two 355 

CAH3 localization sites, and why is CAH3 localized to both regions rather than just one? One 356 

possibility is that the two sites are specialized for different availabilities of CAH3's substrates, H +

tubule -minitubule interface likely operates with abundant substrates, whereas CAH3 in the 359 

reticulated region may operate with lower substrate concentrations, if a fraction of the substrates 360 

is depleted as they pass over the CAH3 at the cylindrical tubule -minitubule interface. 361

in promoting CAH3 localization and potentially function. The extended reach of minitubules along 365 

nearly the full length of the cylindrical tubules may thus be a consequence of their contribution to 366

In conclusion, our work identifies RBMP2 as a key factor mediating the biogenesis of the pyrenoid 377 

reticulated region, revealing how specialized internal membranes are constructed to position 378 

CAH3 for CO ₂ release near Rubisco. The discovery that CAH3 also associates with the 379 

minitubule –cylindrical -tubule interface suggests that multiple membrane environments within the 380 

pyrenoid may contribute to efficient CO ₂ delivery, and points to a previously unappreciated role 381 

for minitubules in CAH3 function. These findings contribute design principles for reconstructing 382 

pyrenoid membrane architecture in crops and establish the pyrenoid as a powerful model for 383 

membrane remodeling within phase -separated condensates. 384
