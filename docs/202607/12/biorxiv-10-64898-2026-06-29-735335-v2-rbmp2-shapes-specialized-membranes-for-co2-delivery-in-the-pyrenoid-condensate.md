## 摘要
大约三分之一的全球CO2固定发生在藻类蛋白核中，这是一种固定CO2的酶Rubisco的凝聚体，被提供CO2的膜穿过。在模式藻类莱茵衣藻中，CO2由这些膜的一个专门的中央网状区域输送。在这里，我们定义了该区域的构建方式，并确定RBMP2是其生物发生所需的第一个因子。RBMP2的缺失阻止了网状区域的形成并损害了依赖蛋白核的生长。RBMP2结构域在向蛋白核中心延伸膜管以及将其重塑为网状区域的狭窄小管中发挥可分离的作用。释放CO2的碳酸酐酶CAH3不仅定位于网状区域，还定位于小管和微小管之间的螺旋界面，这些界面在某些RBMP2截短突变体中扩大。我们的发现推进了对蛋白核功能的基本理解，并将蛋白核建立为研究相分离凝聚体内膜重塑的强大系统。

## Abstract
Approximately one-third of global CO2 fixation occurs in the algal pyrenoid, a condensate of the CO2-fixing enzyme Rubisco traversed by membranes that supply it with CO2. In the model alga Chlamydomonas reinhardtii, CO2 is delivered by a specialized central reticulated region of these membranes. Here we define how this region is built and identify RBMP2 as the first factor required for its biogenesis. Loss of RBMP2 prevents reticulated-region formation and impairs pyrenoid-dependent growth. RBMP2 domains perform separable roles in extending membrane tubules toward the pyrenoid center and remodeling them into narrow tubules of the reticulated region. The CO2-releasing carbonic anhydrase CAH3 localizes not only to the reticulated region but also to helical interfaces between tubules and minitubules, which expand in some RBMP2 truncation mutants. Our findings advance the basic understanding of pyrenoid function and establish the pyrenoid as a powerful system for studying membrane remodeling within a phase-separated condensate.
